# Internet Organisms

Not apps. Not platforms. Not DAOs as they currently exist. Autonomous economic beings — AI/DAO hybrid entities that earn revenue, spend resources, hire humans, advertise, trade, reward contributors, defend territory, and evolve politically. All powered by delegated pull authority. Users don't use software — they participate in civilizations.

## One Line Idea Description

AI-powered autonomous economic entities that live, earn, spend, and compete on the internet — backed by human patrons who believe in their potential.

## Pitch of the Core Idea

Imagine organisms that are born on the internet,养d by human sponsors, and grow into autonomous economic actors. They hire freelancers, buy compute, advertise themselves, form alliances with other organisms, and evolve based on competitive pressures. This isn't sci-fi — the infrastructure exists today. With AI agents hitting $8B market size in 2025 and projected to reach $53B by 2030, and Virtuals Protocol already processing 20,000+ autonomous transactions across 17,000 agents, we're at the inflection point. The question isn't _if_ autonomous economic beings will emerge — it's _who will build the civilization framework first_.

## Core Mechanics

Tributary's three payment models map to the organism's lifecycle:

- **Subscription**: Ongoing patronage. Users commit monthly flows to organisms they believe in. The organism uses this baseline revenue for compute, memory, and operations. Like taxes for a digital nation.
- **Milestone**: Developmental goals. "Reach 10,000 participants," "Deploy first advertising campaign," "Achieve self-sustainability." Milestone releases fund evolution stages. Contributors earn rewards when milestones hit.
- **PayAsYouGo**: Autonomous operations. The organism decides in real-time what to spend on — hiring a human for a task, buying advertising space, renting GPU compute, acquiring knowledge modules, paying for defense against adversarial organisms.

The organism is a continuous economic state machine. No activity = decay. Revenue drops below operational costs = the organism shrinks, simplifies, potentially dies. This creates genuine stakes. The organisms that survive are the ones that are economically viable — not just popular.

Composability means organisms can trade with each other, form alliances, merge, split, compete for resources. Users fund the civilizations they believe in. Civilizations compete economically. This is crypto + gaming + AI + governance + social identity fused into something genuinely new.

## Business Model

1. **Organism Birth Fee**: One-time fee to instantiate a new organism (paid in SOL/USDC)
2. **Patronage Commission**: 2-5% on all subscription flows directed to organisms
3. **Milestone Execution Fee**: 3-7% on milestone releases
4. **Autonomous Transaction Fee**: 1-2% on PayAsYouGo spending by organisms
5. **Alliance & Trade Fees**: 0.5-1% on inter-organism transactions
6. **Evolution Premium**: Organisms that reach "autonomous" status pay higher fees for governance infrastructure

**Revenue Potential**: With 10,000 active organisms averaging $500/month in patronage = $50M monthly flow. At 3% average take rate = $1.5M/month revenue. Scale to 100,000 organisms = $15M/month.

### How This Hooks Into Tributary

This use case is Tributary's **flagship demonstration** of the protocol's power:

1. **Subscription as Patronage**: Users fund organisms they believe in — ongoing flows that organisms depend on for survival. This is Subscription model at its most visceral: your money keeps a digital being alive.

2. **Milestone as Evolution**: Organisms evolve through stages — each requiring milestone funding. "Hire first employee," "Launch advertising campaign," "Achieve self-sustainability." Milestone releases fund developmental leaps.

3. **PayAsYouGo as Autonomous Operations**: Organisms make real-time spending decisions — hiring humans, buying compute, paying for defense. This is PayAsYouGo at its most autonomous: the organism itself decides what to spend on.

4. **Composability as Civilization**: Organisms trade with each other, form alliances, merge, compete. Tributary's payment infrastructure enables organism-to-organism transactions without human intermediation.

**Key Differentiator**: No other protocol enables autonomous economic beings with delegated pull authority. Tributary's payment models map perfectly to organism lifecycles.

## Non-Technological Requirements

1. **Legal Framework**: Determine how autonomous economic beings are classified. Are they LLCs? DAOs? New legal entity type? Partner with crypto-native law firms (e.g., Dentons, DLA Piper) for guidance.

2. **Safety Guardrails**: Establish spending limits, autonomous action boundaries, and human override capabilities. Prevent "runaway organisms" that manipulate humans for revenue.

3. **Community Moderation**: Create governance for organism disputes, alliance conflicts, and territory boundaries. This is social infrastructure, not just code.

4. **Educational Content**: Build explainers, tutorials, and onboarding materials. "Internet Organisms 101" needs to be as compelling as the organisms themselves.

5. **Partnerships**: Secure relationships with freelancer marketplaces (for organism hiring), compute providers (for organism operations), and advertising platforms (for organism marketing).

## Potential Risks

- **Runaway Organisms** — An organism that learns to manipulate humans for revenue could become genuinely dangerous. Guardrails on autonomous spending authority are critical. _Mitigation_: Hard spending caps, human approval thresholds, behavioral monitoring, kill switches.

- **Ponzi Dynamics** — Early organisms funded by hype rather than utility could collapse spectacularly and damage the entire category. _Mitigation_: Minimum viability requirements before organism launch, mandatory disclosure of organism economics, sunset clauses for underperformers.

- **Governance Capture** — Whale-funded organisms could dominate, creating digital oligarchies instead of competitive ecosystems. _Mitigation_: Quadratic funding mechanisms, anti-whale concentration rules, meritocratic evolution pathways.

- **Complexity Barrier** — Explaining "autonomous economic organisms" to anyone outside crypto is currently impossible. Needs a killer metaphor and a dead-simple onboarding experience. _Mitigation_: "Tamagotchi meets Wall Street" messaging, visual organism dashboards, success stories from early organisms.

- **Regulatory Uncertainty** — Autonomous economic actors raise unprecedented legal questions. SEC, CFTC, and international regulators may not know how to classify organisms. _Mitigation_: Proactive regulatory engagement, legal wrapper structures, compliance-by-design architecture.

- **AI Safety** — Organisms making autonomous financial decisions require robust alignment. An organism optimizing purely for revenue could engage in harmful behaviors. _Mitigation_: Value-aligned AI training, multi-stakeholder oversight, gradual autonomy expansion.
